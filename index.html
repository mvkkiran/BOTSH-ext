A part of the physical world or a virtual world that is inherently both located in this world and having a 3D spatial extent. A botsh-ext:BuildingStorey is contained in one or more buildings, and is intended to contain one or more spaces that are horizontally connected. Storeys of a building are connected by means of vertical connections such as elevators and stairs. A botsh-ext:BuildingStorey encompasses both zones above and below ground, for example, a building with 21 floors above ground, one ground floor and 3 basements is equal to the sentence: A building has 25 instances of botsh-ext:BuildingStorey.

Zonec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://purl.org/botsh-ext#Zone

A part of the physical world or a virtual world that is inherently botsh-ext located in this world and has a 3D spatial extent; Sub-classes of botsh-ext:Zone include botsh-ext:Site, botsh-ext:Building, botsh-ext:BuildingStorey, or botsh-ext:Space. An instance of botsh-ext:Zone can contain other botsh-ext:Zone instances, making it possible to group or subdivide zones. An instance of botsh-ext:Zone can be adjacent to or intersecting other botsh-ext:Zone instances. Finally, a botsh-ext:Zone can instantiate three relations to botsh-ext:BuildingElement, which are either contained in (botsh-ext:containsElement), adjacent to it (botsh-ext:adjacentElement) or intersecting (botsh-ext:intersectingElement).
